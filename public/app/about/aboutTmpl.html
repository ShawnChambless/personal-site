About Me

I grew up in Moultrie, Georgia. After high school, I enlisted in the U.S. Navy as an Aviation Ordnanceman. During my enlistment, I had the privilege of being stationed in Jacksonville, FL, Lemoore, CA, Atsugi, Japan, as well as some short port visits in France, Dubai, Australia, Singapore, Philippines, and China.

After leaving the Navy, I moved to Vermont to be closer to my mother's side of the family. Although beautiful, the Vermont economy was less than ideal at the time, and eventually I had to get a job as a Correctional Officer. On the first day of the Vermont State Correctional Academy, I met the girl who would become my wife, Angela. After working at the correctional facility for about a year and a half, we decided to go to college at the University of Alaska Anchorage. We packed up her Kia Soul, and hit the road for a 9 day trip to Anchorage.

While in Anchorage, I attended the University of Alaska Anchorage studying Aviation Maintenance. At some point during the 2 years in Vermont, I picked up homebrewing beer. The only logical progression from beer was soap, so I made an excessive amount of just ok soap. My next thought was that I could sell my soap, so I decided to try my hand at making a website. After finishing the HTML codecademy course, I became obsessed with web development. I began researching and practicing every waking moment instead of listening to lectures and doing homework. Finally, I applied to DevMountain - a 13 week Full Stack dev-bootcamp in Provo, UT.

I spent the month after applying to DevMountain anxiously awaiting (read: checking my email approximately every 2 to 3 minutes) the email that would hopefully be my acceptance into the 13 week web dev immersive course. The day that I finally got the email, I was in the middle of replacing an engine on a Cessna 172. I had to stop and literally make a lap in the hangar excitedly telling everyone that I got in. I couldn't believe that my dream had finally come true. I couldn't believe that I was about to embark on a 3 month journey toward my dream career. When the time finally came, I packed up my car and began my second trip on the Alaska Highway.

I am currently seeking opportunities in web development in Atlanta, GA.
